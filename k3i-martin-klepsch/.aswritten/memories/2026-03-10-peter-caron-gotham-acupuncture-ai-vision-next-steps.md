# Onboarding Call Part 3 — Peter Caron, Gotham Acupuncture: AI Vision and Next Steps

Final section of the discovery call. Covers Peter's existing AI experimentation, specific use cases he identified for aswritten.ai, his strong buy-in, pricing discussion, and next steps including bringing Jordan Barber onto the next call.

## Peter's Existing AI Experimentation

Peter is already hands-on with AI tooling despite being primarily a practitioner:

> "I've already been experimenting with running N8N and LM Studio on my laptop and already have set up a couple like processes and systems that have been helpful."

Jordan Barber's pre-acupuncture career was in IT, making him the more technical partner:

> "Thankfully Jordan's background pre acupuncture was in IT. So this is an area that he's more advanced by far than I am."

## Local Infrastructure and HIPAA Strategy

The plan is to run LLMs locally on a practice server to handle HIPAA compliance:

> "We're looking at setting up a server within this new practice that runs all these LLMs locally. Locally, which would get us around a lot of HIPAA issues if it's not connected to the Internet. We actually all of a sudden can do all sorts of crazy stuff with the data because it's not going anywhere."

Peter sees data security as a premium feature for their high-net-worth clientele:

> "Part of our pitch is going to be like we are more secure than HIPAA. Like we're dealing with high net worth individuals on the Upper West Side. I already have a few people who are like one of the rooms we're setting up is going to be like the VIP treatment room where it's like you skip the waiting room if you're a celebrity, if you're whatever."

> "If we can say like highly encrypted, locally managed data, I think there's a space for. I think you understand what I'm saying."

Scarlet noted that aswritten.ai is designed for on-premises deployment and that the models she uses have zero data retention guarantees. Peter raised that HIPAA has rules about data transmission itself, not just storage.

## AI Use Case: Narrative Charting

Peter identified narrative-based charting as a key AI application he's excited about:

> "This is actually like one of the parts of AIs that I'm really excited about is the ability to do narrative explanation. So this is one of the things we've been looking at for chart generation is the ability to kind of sit down and just ramble about the patient for three to five minutes, like including the technical information, but also talking about like, I don't know, something seems off about them today, they seem kind of down or like their posture seems a little worse and just have all of this get fed into like a consistent chart format."

## AI Use Case: Training Manual Generation

Peter's key insight — use recorded training sessions to generate cited training manuals:

> "Let's say we have like the one that's the business and the one that's the medicine side. The medicine side. This sounds like I could be training students in person and just have like a phone recording all the text. And then over the course of, say, 30 hours of training students on migraines, I could then generate a migraine training manual out of this."

> "And it would be able to cite the specific places where this happens."

Scarlet confirmed: "It would cite individual conversation, individual training sessions that you recorded and then uploaded as transcripts."

## AI Use Case: Segmented Access for New Hires

Peter immediately grasped the segmentation model:

> "I could set this up such that one of the new hires could use Claude from a home laptop to link into one specific section of the entire worldview of the business. That's say, just on migraine care. They could have conversations and study this from home."

Scarlet confirmed the capability exists but noted: "The segmentation like you're talking about right now needs a little massaging for the UI to work fluidly, but 100% exists."

## AI Use Case: Doctor Relationship Memory

Peter identified using collective memory to track individual doctor relationships:

> "If I'm doing a call with a doctor, I can record that whole call and feed it into this and build a database of what doctor communication looks like, even for specific doctors. So it's like, reference every call with Dr. John Doe for, you know, oh, how's your kid doing? How's your. This, how's your. You know, so there could be like almost like having like something that kind of universally remembers whatever we feed it."

## Two Worldviews: Business vs. Clinical

Scarlet identified that Gotham Acupuncture would likely need two separate worldviews:

> "There's the organizational worldview as in the organization as a business and then the organization as a healthcare entity that is tracking patient treatments and all of this stuff connected to it. I think is really fascinating. You absolutely could use this kind of tool for both, whether it's a single worldview or that's two and they're sort of interconnected I think is sort of maybe an open question."

Scarlet suggested that HIPAA issues could be sidestepped initially by not using it for patient data: "You could probably neatly sidestep the HIPAA issues by not using it for patient stuff in the beginning."

## Strong Buy-In

Peter expressed clear enthusiasm and immediate understanding of value:

> "Scarlett, this is pretty neat, what you built here. I mean, I see the usefulness of it basically immediately. Especially because it sounds like as long as we're capturing data well, this doesn't necessarily need a ton of direct training."

> "I would love to use this. This looks great."

> "You've definitely got a good business here."

## Pricing Discussion

Scarlet outlined the pricing model:

> "Right now, the organizational pricing is $400 a month, which includes unlimited team members. And on top of that, I'm providing consulting services where I do this kind of knowledge extraction of you and other teammates and also manage the document entry. And that can be one time, that can also be ongoing, depending on how much you'd like me to be involved in like making sure the system sort of continues working with you. And then I can also provide services to do things like custom ontologies like you mentioned, or like automations on top of it. Say you want to automatically have every call that comes into your automated transcription thing get saved as a memory. That doesn't take very long, but that's the kind of thing I bill hourly for on top."

Peter deferred pricing discussion to after Jordan sees the demo: "We'll talk about that part with Jordan after he gets the whole pitch, the whole thing next week."
